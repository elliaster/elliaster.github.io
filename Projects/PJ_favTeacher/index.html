Biography

Early Life
 Jorge Luis Cordero Jr., born on November 30th 1958 in Brooklyn, is the son of Jorge Luis Cordero and as his father taught him art and became a major influence that spearheaded his passion and ambition to become an artist. He was surrounded by art as his mother was a poet who would recite poems from memory or improvise in the moment, and his father, previously mentioned. He grew up in Sackett Street. He started working at the age of 10 delivering groceries. By the time he was 13 he worked at a factory, the same place his grandfather and father before him worked, he did that job until he was 17.

Education
 He attended the High School of Art and Design where he studied under great artists/teachers such as Max Ginsburg and Irwin Greenberg. From all the opportunities he had he was able to create an impressive portfolio that got him into Parsons School of Design. He had received a partial scholarship and later earned another scholarship, this time to go to Paris, France where he studied archaeology. He later was selected to participate in a project to inspire landscape painters. He says he was able to study under many great artists, but one in particular had a major influence, his name was Roger Shepard. "He is a true Renaissance man. He inspired me to also be a Renaissance man, scholar and educator."

Other Interests/ Skills
 He is interested in, or is (a): Musician of percussion, guitar and vocal instruments, culinary arts, creative writing, fine arts of painting, drawing, sculpture, and mural painting.

Career

 In his career Cordero has made a great deal of accomplishments. He was featured on NBC news for the creation of the Painted Doors titled, "Doors of Hope", which was created for the children of New Orleans, Hurricane Katrina victims, in collaboration with CITYARTS 2009. He has been recognized by the New York Times as an outstanding educator in 1997, as well as featured in a book by Mario Asaro, "Pluralistic Approaches to Art Criticism". He had his art exhibited at, The Kenny Gallery in Ireland, Parsons Gallery, Studio Museum of Harlem, Museum of Modern Art, Taller Boricau Gallery, and El Museo del Barrio, previous five are all situated in New York.
 He works as a New York City Board of Education Visual Arts & Art History Instructor from September 2004 to Present. Cordero has worked in Washington Irving High School/Art House as a Visual Arts Instructor, he would do this for several other schools, and academies before and after 2001.

Impact On Me

 Jorge Luis Cordero, known to me as Mr. Cordero, has made a big impact on me as I am very interested in art, and up until high school I didn't have an art class that interested me, where as Mr. Cordero had. He helped me get back into drawing and let me realize how much fun art is. His class is one of the most enjoyable classes I have ever had the pleasure of taking. I appreciate what he has done for me. If not for him I would not be actively trying to better myself at art.
